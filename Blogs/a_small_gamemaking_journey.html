A SMALL GAMEMAKING JOURNEY

30/09/2022

After releasing the demo of my latest game, Spring Escape, I decided to spend some time learning Gamemaker features that I've been putting on the learning backlog for a long while in the hopes of using them to make my games even better. Here's a list of what I've learned so far:

3D in Gamemaker

3D in Gamemaker has always been something that has fascinated me ever since I was making stuff in 8.1, the fact people can take an engine that offers so little tools for making those kind of games easily is amazing, and I wanted to try it too.
I followed DragoniteSpam's 3D in Gamemaker series to understand the basics and so far I managed to get a very simple character controller and some basic models working.

In the future I will explore more 3D related things like collisions, I really want to make some kind of little game with this.

Shaders

I already knew some of the basics of shaders, but only really how to make a sprite turn completely white, and for most of the time I would just use other people's shaders and hope that they worked correctly. This time I decided to learn them in depth, especially things like texture samplers and coordinates, as well as learning how to make things like pallete swaps, displacement, and more. I ended up making many little fun tests with them.



Buffers

The next thing I wanted to learn was buffer, an interesting way to store data that I've never tried before. I think the best part of them is being able to take color data from surfaces and easily check a color of every pixel, it lead to many interesting experiments.
My first test was trying to recreate aarthificial's "Reinvented Pixel Art Animation" system, a very interesting way of animating pixel art. While the system isn't perfect, it was still a very fun experiment.
Then, I tried doing a small Splatoon like game in 2D, using buffers to determine how much ink each team has placed.


And this is it for what I've learned during this month, lots of interesting stuff for sure, and probably quite a lot for one month. I'll now go back to solely focus on Spring Escape, but there's still a lot of Gamemaker things I still have to learn!
